Why Nairobi is Perfect for a Family Holiday

Nairobi is one of the world’s most unique capitals – the only city with a national park on its doorstep. Families can enjoy a genuine safari with the city skyline in the background. The city also boasts world‑class animal sanctuaries: the Giraffe Centre and David Sheldrick Elephant Orphanage offer close‑up encounters that children will never forget.

Beyond wildlife, Nairobi has interactive museums (Nairobi National Museum, Railway Museum), cultural villages like Bomas of Kenya, and plenty of green spaces. The city is well‑served by family‑friendly hotels with pools, kids’ menus, and interconnecting rooms. With good infrastructure and safe tour operators, Nairobi makes an excellent start or end to any Kenyan family adventure.

Top Family Activities in Nairobi

Giraffe Centre

Get eye‑to‑eye with endangered Rothschild giraffes. Kids can feed them from a raised platform – a magical experience. Educational talk included. Open daily.

Sheldrick Elephant Orphanage

Watch baby elephants being fed and playing in the mud. Heartwarming rescue stories. Open 11am–12pm daily. Booking essential. Kids love the keepers.

Nairobi National Park

Go on a morning or afternoon game drive – see lions, zebras, giraffes, and rhinos with skyscrapers in the background. Perfect for a half‑day family safari.

Nairobi National Museum

Interactive natural history, prehistory, and art exhibits. Kids love the “Cradle of Humankind” and the snake park. Half‑day fun, indoors and shaded.

Family Dining

Nairobi has many child‑friendly restaurants. Try The Carnivore (game meat, kids’ buffet), About Thyme (garden setting, play area), or Jiko for local cuisine.

Sample 4‑Day Family Itinerary for Nairobi

Day 1

Arrival & Giraffe Centre

Arrive at Jomo Kenyatta International Airport. Private transfer to your family hotel. Afternoon: visit the Giraffe Centre – feed and photograph the giraffes. Dinner at the hotel or a nearby family restaurant.

Day 2

Elephant Orphanage & Nairobi National Park

Morning: Sheldrick Elephant Orphanage (11am feeding). Afternoon: half‑day game drive in Nairobi National Park – see lions, rhinos, and zebras. Picnic lunch inside the park.

Day 3

Museum & Cultural Experience

Morning: Nairobi National Museum (snake park included). Afternoon: free time for shopping at Maasai Market or relaxing at the hotel pool. Evening: dinner at The Carnivore.

Day 4

Railway Museum & Departure

Morning: Nairobi Railway Museum – vintage trains, children can climb into old locomotives. Last souvenir shopping. Transfer to airport for flight home.

🧸 Family Travel Tips – Nairobi

  • Best time: June–October (dry, pleasant) and December–March (warm). Avoid long rains (April–May).
  • Getting around: Use Uber/Bolt or hotel‑arranged transfers – safer and easier with kids. Avoid matatus.
  • Safety: Nairobi is generally safe for tourists in well‑known areas. Always supervise children, especially near roads.
  • Stroller friendly: Museums and Giraffe Centre are stroller‑friendly; Nairobi National Park requires a vehicle (4x4).
  • Packing: Sunscreen, hats, insect repellent, light jackets for evenings, and comfortable walking shoes.
  • Health: Yellow fever vaccination required for entry (from some countries). Malaria prophylaxis recommended if visiting parks.

💰 Family Budget for Nairobi (2026)

  • Accommodation (family of 4, mid‑range hotel): €180–€350 per night
  • Meals (breakfast included, lunch & dinner out): €35–€60 per day
  • Activities (Giraffe Centre, Elephant Orphanage, National Park): €20–€45 per person per day
  • Transport (Uber/taxi): €15–€30 per day
  • Total daily budget (family of 4): €270–€485

💡 Money‑Saving Tips for Families

  • Many hotels offer free stays for children under 12 – always check before booking.
  • Book the Elephant Orphanage online in advance – limited slots but affordable (donation).
  • Combine Nairobi National Park with a picnic lunch to save on restaurant costs.
  • Use Uber for short trips – cheaper than hotel taxis.
  • Consider accommodation with breakfast included and a kitchenette for simple meals.
