How Much Does a Watamu Holiday Cost?

A Watamu holiday costs between €50 and €500+ per day depending on your travel style. Our all‑inclusive luxury package starts at €279 per day (minimum 5 nights) and includes everything you need for a stress‑free beach escape: 5‑star beachfront hotel, private transfers, marine park access, and 24/7 concierge.

For independent travellers, a mid‑range budget (3‑star hotel, local restaurants, tuk‑tuks) is around €120–€200 per day. Backpackers can manage on €50–€80/day using guesthouses and street food.

💸 Hidden Costs Tourists Miss

  • Marine park fees – often excluded from boat quotes (add $20)
  • Card surcharges – 3‑5% at most hotels; bring shillings
  • ATM withdrawal fees – 500 KES + 5% forex
  • "Beach boy" markups – bargain to 40‑50% of first price

📅 Cheapest Months to Visit Watamu

  • May – June (long rains) – 40‑50% hotel discounts
  • November (short rains) – affordable and fewer tourists
  • Avoid December‑January & Easter for best rates

Frequently Asked Questions — Watamu Holiday Costs

Is Watamu expensive for tourists? ▾
Not at all. A couple can enjoy a mid‑range holiday for €120–€220/day, which is cheaper than Zanzibar or Diani. Backpackers manage on €50/day. Our luxury package from €279/day offers exceptional value with private transfers and 5‑star hotels.
How much is a meal in Watamu? ▾
Local meal: 500–1,200 KES (€4–9). Seafood dinner: 2,000–4,000 KES (€15–31). Italian dinner: 1,500–3,000 KES (€12–23).
Is Watamu cheaper than Zanzibar? ▾
Yes – by about 30%. Accommodation and food in Watamu are significantly less expensive, and marine park fees are lower.
How much spending money for 1 week in Watamu? ▾
For mid‑range independent travel (excluding accommodation), budget €800–€1,500 for two people. Our all‑inclusive package covers almost everything except lunch and souvenirs.
