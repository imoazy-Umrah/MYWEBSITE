A Legacy of Adventure – Kenya’s Tourism Story

Kenya has been a magnet for explorers and travellers for over a century. In the early 1900s, European aristocrats arrived by steamship and the newly built Uganda Railway to hunt big game, establishing the first safari camps. By the 1930s, writers such as Ernest Hemingway and Karen Blixen (author of Out of Africa) immortalised Kenya’s wild landscapes, attracting a steady flow of international visitors.

After independence in 1963, Kenya became a pioneer in sustainable wildlife tourism. Nairobi National Park – the only national park on the edge of a capital city – was already protected. In 1977, Kenya banned all hunting, shifting its focus entirely to photographic safaris and eco‑tourism. Today, Kenya welcomes over two million tourists annually, making it the leading safari destination in East Africa.

From the palm‑fringed shores of Watamu and Diani to the endless plains of the Masai Mara, Kenya’s combination of pristine beaches, diverse wildlife, and rich cultural heritage remains unmatched. How Much Does a Kenya Holiday Cost? (Quick Answer)

A Kenya holiday costs between €50 and €600+ per day depending on your travel style. For a 7‑night beach + safari combo (e.g., 4 nights Watamu + 3 nights Tsavo East), a couple should budget:

  • Budget (camping, self‑catering): €1,500 – €2,200 total
  • Mid‑range (3‑star hotels, local restaurants): €2,800 – €4,500 total
  • Luxury (our all‑inclusive package): €5,000 – €9,000 total

Kenya is more affordable than Tanzania or Zanzibar for similar experiences, and significantly cheaper than South Africa's safari circuit.

💸 Hidden Costs Tourists Often Miss

  • Marine park fees – often excluded from boat trip quotes (add €15–€20)
  • Card surcharges – 3‑5% at many hotels; carry shillings or cash (Euros/USD)
  • ATM withdrawal fees – 500 KES + 5% forex
  • “Beach boy” markups – bargain to 40‑50% of the first price
  • Park entry fees for non‑residents – Tsavo: €52/day, Masai Mara: €80/day

💰 How to Save on Kenya Travel

  • Use local SIM (Safaricom) – 1GB for €1.50 (200 KES)
  • Eat at local “hoteli” – chapati + beans = €2–€3
  • Book safaris directly with local operators (not international agents) – save 20‑30%
  • Take the SGR train from Nairobi to Mombasa (economy €8, first class €23) rather than flying
  • Tuk‑tuk instead of taxi – 80% cheaper for short trips

Frequently Asked Questions – Kenya Travel Costs

Is Kenya expensive for tourists? ▾
No – mid‑range travellers spend €120–€220 per day, cheaper than Zanzibar or South Africa. Backpackers manage on €50/day. Our luxury packages start at €279/day all‑inclusive.

Tsavo East: €150–€280 (budget camping) / €280–€500 (mid‑range lodge) / €500+ (luxury). Masai Mara is 30‑50% higher, especially during the Great Migration (July–October).
How much is a meal in Kenya? ▾
Local meal (kibanda): €2–€4. Casual restaurant: €8–€15. Fine dining: €20–€40.
Is Kenya cheaper than Zanzibar? ▾
Yes – about 30% cheaper.
What is the cheapest month to visit Kenya? ▾

How much spending money for 1 week in Kenya? ▾
Mid‑range independent (excl. accommodation): €300–€600. Our packages are all‑inclusive.
Do I need a visa for Kenya? ▾
Most nationalities need an eTA. Apply at etakenya.go.ke. Fee $32.50.
