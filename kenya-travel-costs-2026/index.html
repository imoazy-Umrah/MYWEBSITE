Kenya: Africa's Most Diverse Travel Destination

Kenya offers an extraordinary range of experiences that few countries can match. In a single trip, you can snorkel over coral reefs in the morning, watch elephants roam beneath Mount Kilimanjaro in the afternoon, and dine in a world-class restaurant in Nairobi by evening.

This guide brings together everything you need to know about Kenya's top destinations – from the historic streets of Mombasa to the wildlife-rich plains of Tsavo East, the pristine beaches of Diani and Watamu to the urban energy of Nairobi. Each section includes insider tips, local prices, and practical advice from our team at Bwanaadi Tours – a family-owned business with 20+ years of experience on the Kenyan coast.

Sample 10-Day Kenya Itinerary

The perfect beach-safari-city loop, tried and tested by hundreds of travellers.

Day 1-2 Mombasa – Beach & History

Arrive at Moi International Airport. Transfer to a beachfront hotel on Nyali or Diani Beach. Visit Fort Jesus, wander through Old Town, and enjoy sunset at Mama Ngina Drive. Budget: €80–€150/day mid‑range.

Day 3-4 Tsavo East Safari – Red Elephants

Drive 2.5 hours to Tsavo East National Park (entry $80). Morning and evening game drives to spot red elephants, lions, giraffes. Visit Mudanda Rock and Lugard Falls. Budget: €280–€450/night all inclusive lodge.

Day 5-7 Watamu – Marine Park & Dolphin Watching

Return to the coast for pure relaxation. Snorkel in Watamu Marine Park (entry $20). Join a sunset dolphin cruise. Explore Gede Ruins and Marafa Depression. Budget: €120–€220/day mid‑range.

Day 8-9 Nairobi – City & Wildlife

Fly from Malindi to Nairobi (€120–180). Visit Giraffe Centre, Sheldrick Elephant Orphanage (11am only), Karen Blixen Museum, and enjoy dinner at Carnivore Restaurant. Budget: €80–€140/day.

Day 10 Departure – Nairobi National Park or Fly Home

Optional early morning game drive at Nairobi National Park ($80) before transferring to Jomo Kenyatta International Airport for your flight home. Total trip (mid‑range, excluding flights): ~€3,800 for two people.

Frequently Asked Questions — Kenya Travel

Do I need a visa for Kenya? ▾
Yes – most nationalities require an electronic Travel Authorisation (eTA). Apply online at etakenya.go.ke. Fee: $32.50. Processing takes 1-3 business days.
Is Kenya safe for tourists? ▾
Yes – tourist areas like Watamu, Diani, Mombasa, Nairobi, and the national parks are generally safe. Use common sense: avoid walking alone at night, keep valuables hidden, and use registered taxis or Uber.
What's the currency and can I use cards? ▾
Kenyan Shilling (KES). €1 ≈ 145 KES, $1 ≈ 135 KES. Cards accepted at hotels and upscale restaurants, but expect 3-5% surcharges. Cash is king for markets, tuk-tuks, and small eateries.
Do I need vaccinations? ▾
Yellow fever vaccination is required if travelling from an endemic country. Recommended: Typhoid, Hepatitis A & B, and malaria prophylaxis. Consult a travel clinic 4-6 weeks before departure.
What language do they speak? ▾
Swahili (Kiswahili) is the national language, but English is widely spoken in tourist areas, hotels, and restaurants.
Can I drink tap water? ▾
No – stick to bottled or filtered water. Most hotels provide complimentary bottled water. Avoid ice cubes at street stalls.
